Utility Location & Line Exposure

Safe, Precise Underground Infrastructure Detection and Exposure

What is Utility Location & Line Exposure?

Utility location and line exposure is a specialized service that uses safe, precise pneumatic excavation to expose underground utilities without damage. This non-invasive technique allows for accurate identification, verification, and assessment of buried infrastructure including water lines, gas pipes, electrical conduits, telecommunications cables, and irrigation systems before excavation or construction activities.

Air excavation provides the safest method for exposing utilities because it gently removes soil without the cutting, crushing, or puncturing risks associated with mechanical digging. This precision protects critical infrastructure, prevents service interruptions, and ensures compliance with safety regulations.

Benefits of Air Excavation for Utility Location

  • Zero Utility Damage: Pneumatic excavation cannot cut or puncture underground lines, eliminating the risk of costly utility strikes
  • Precise Location Verification: Confirms exact depth, routing, and condition of underground utilities before construction begins
  • Safety Compliance: Meets OSHA and local safety requirements for excavation near underground utilities
  • Cost Savings: Avoiding utility strikes prevents expensive repairs, service interruptions, and potential fines
  • Project Risk Reduction: Accurate utility information allows for better planning and reduces construction delays
  • Visual Inspection Capability: Exposed utilities can be assessed for condition, corrosion, or maintenance needs
  • Safe for All Utility Types: Effective for gas, electric, water, sewer, telecommunications, and irrigation systems
  • Minimal Surface Disruption: Precise excavation creates small access points that are easily restored
  • Fast and Efficient: Quick exposure allows projects to proceed on schedule without costly delays
  • Documentation Support: Provides accurate utility mapping information for project planning and future reference

Our Process

  1. Pre-Excavation Planning

    We review utility maps, call 811 for utility marking, and identify the specific locations requiring exposure and verification.

  2. Site Preparation

    The excavation area is prepared and nearby features are protected to ensure safe, controlled operations.

  3. Electronic Utility Detection

    We use advanced locating equipment to identify the approximate position and depth of underground utilities.

  4. Precision Air Excavation

    Using pneumatic technology, we carefully remove soil to expose utilities without any risk of damage to lines or conduits.

  5. Utility Documentation

    Exposed utilities are photographed, measured, and documented for project planning and permanent records.

  6. Condition Assessment

    If requested, we assess utility condition and identify any maintenance or replacement needs before backfilling.

  7. Safe Backfilling and Restoration

    Excavation areas are carefully backfilled and restored to match surrounding conditions, protecting exposed utilities.
